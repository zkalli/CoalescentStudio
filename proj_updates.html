The latest of our current work

2/11/2020: Near the Minimum Viable Product

We are nearing our first milestone - a working minimum viable product by February 14. We are slightly behind schedule due to programming delays, but should still be able to complete our user testing by February 21st. We are shifting the controls over to the gamepad, which is presenting some setbacks, but are working through it. Spencer has been working on the core animations for the blacksmith and the rig for the enemy tree, so neither are entirely complete yet, but both are close. Anything we might be lacking for the MVP we will use a placeholder animation for.

We had a design discussion in which we determined we would move the blacksmith workshop from the top to the bottom of the screen. This has put UI art behind, because we want to re-evaluate it. However, Kalli has finished the enemy tree model and texture, as well as props (sword, hammer, and anvil). Our team is finishing up that work now to ensure we are ready to start playtesting next week!

    • 1/27/2020: Programming Hold Ups

    The smith model is complete and the texture is being polished.The smith rig is also done and animation is beginning soon! We will begin with just the core animations for the smith needed for the minimum viable product (walk, run, action, etc.). Art is progressing as planned, but programming is hitting some bumps in the road. After a discussion with Varun, the programming schedule has been shrunk - the final deadline has not been moved. He is busy getting graduate school applications in by January 31st, and he will be ramping up development after that. Still, progress is being made slowly on the game mechanics. We’ve begun work on the tower placement that the blacksmith will be able to do, and Varun will continue to focus on this mechanic while he works with Brantly McCord (a very helpful teaching assistant) to hammer out some issues with the crafting mechanic (pun certainly intended).

    Besides that, we’ve been marketing on Twitter and got more information about publishing a game on Steam from some Purdue students who have done it before. Those proceedings will begin soon, and we will be monitoring our presence on Twitter to see what we can do better.

    • 1/21/2020: Week One, Done

    Our first week back in production is over, and we’ve already got some great work done. Kallista has started and finished the blacksmith character model in record time, and will begin texturing it while Spencer works on getting the model rigged. We are running behind on the programming of the crafting system. Since this is the first major system we are developing for the game, we want to make sure we get it right and define our classes the best way we can to save some headaches later. To account for this, we will be re-evaluating the programming schedule over the next week to come to a solution.

    We’ve also devised a marketing schedule for the next three weeks. We will be focusing on building our Twitter account for now, while also keeping the website up to date. The work we currently have is not in a state to be marketed to the Polytechnic administration or CGT staff and faculty. Once the game is more complete we will advertise to the department for user testing and to spread the word about a more complete product.
